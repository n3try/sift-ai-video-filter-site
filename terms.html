2. What Sift does

Sift reviews visible platform disclosures, creator disclosures, configured watermark matches, and community feedback on supported video pages. It may show an AI signal score, evidence strength, and the signals behind the result.

A Sift result is not proof that a video is AI-generated, false, harmful, or unlawful. Signals can be missing, ambiguous, or wrong. Do not use Sift as the only basis for an accusation or for a decision about employment, credit, housing, insurance, health, legal rights, or personal safety.

4. Community feedback and reputation

AI, human, unsure, and dispute votes are user opinions. A majority vote can be useful context, but it is not proof. Sift may delay, discount, exclude, or reverse votes when necessary to reduce spam, coordinated manipulation, or obvious mistakes.

Voting reputation may affect how much a future vote contributes. Reputation is intended to come from independently resolved outcomes, not simply from agreeing with the current majority. Sift may change the reputation method as abuse patterns and evidence quality become clearer.

By submitting feedback, you allow Sift to store, analyze, aggregate, display, and moderate it for operating, securing, and improving the service.
