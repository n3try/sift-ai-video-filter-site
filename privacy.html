Privacy and data use

What Sift reads, sends, and stores.

Sift processes information needed to evaluate supported YouTube and TikTok pages, return shared classifications, and record feedback. Full videos, audio, screenshots, and unrelated browsing history are not uploaded.

1. Scope

This notice applies to Sift public beta version 0.3.2 and its hosted classification service. Sift runs on supported YouTube and TikTok pages. It is designed to avoid collecting a general browsing history.

Sift identifies possible production signals. It does not determine whether a video is truthful, harmful, or appropriate.

2. Information Sift handles

Read on supported pages

  • Video and creator identifiers.
  • Visible titles, captions, descriptions, hashtags, and platform labels.
  • Low-resolution corner samples when optional local watermark matching is enabled. These samples stay in the browser.

Sent in hosted mode

  • Video identifiers and compact summaries of detected signals.
  • AI, human, unsure, and dispute feedback.
  • A pseudonymous installation key derived from a random identifier stored by the extension.
  • Normal network information received by the API infrastructure for delivery, security, and abuse prevention.

Not uploaded by this beta

  • Full videos, sampled video frames, audio, or screenshots.
  • Raw titles, captions, or descriptions.
  • Unrelated browsing history, personal messages, or passwords.

3. How information is used

Sift uses the information above to return classifications, explain which signals contributed to a result, aggregate viewer feedback, prevent repeated abuse, and maintain the reliability of the beta service.

Sift does not sell personal information or use extension data for advertising.

4. Service providers

The hosted beta uses Cloudflare Workers and Hyperdrive to receive and route API requests, and Neon to operate the PostgreSQL database. These providers process information as needed to run their infrastructure and may receive normal connection data under their own service terms.

The database stores pseudonymous observer values rather than raw IP addresses. Cloudflare still receives IP and connection information when it serves a request.

5. Retention and deletion

Local settings remain in Chrome until you clear the extension data or remove Sift. Shared signal summaries and feedback may remain in the beta database while they are needed for classifications, abuse prevention, or service testing.

Beta limitation

A fixed server-retention schedule is not yet published. If you require a guaranteed deletion period, clear the API URL in Sift settings and use local mode instead of the hosted beta.

Infrastructure logs are controlled by the applicable Cloudflare and Neon service settings and retention practices.

6. Your controls

  • Disable Sift at any time from chrome://extensions.
  • Clear the API base URL in Sift settings to keep classifications and feedback on your device. Shared classifications will not be available in local mode.
  • Remove the extension or clear its storage to delete locally stored settings and identifiers.
  • Contact the maintainer to ask about hosted data or deletion. Server-side requests may require enough information to locate the pseudonymous record.

7. Contact and changes

Sift is maintained through the n3try GitHub account. General questions and data requests can begin through the public support tracker. Do not post installation identifiers or other private information in a public issue. Open a minimal issue to arrange a safer follow-up channel.

Security reports can be submitted privately through GitHub private vulnerability reporting.

This notice applies to the current public beta. Material changes will be recorded here with a new effective date.
