How it works

Multiple signals, one explainable estimate.

Sift does not claim it can magically identify every synthetic video. It combines signals that can be checked and tells you when the evidence is too weak.

01

Platform labels

YouTube and TikTok AI or provenance labels are strong signals, but they are not treated as infallible.

02

Creator disclosures

Captions, descriptions, hashtags, and named generation tools contribute to the estimate.

03

Local watermark checks

Optional low-resolution corner checks run in your browser. Frames are not sent to the Sift API.

04

Community feedback

AI, human, and unsure votes help correct weak or disputed classifications over time.

Questions

Before you install.

Why can’t the button install Sift automatically?

Chrome blocks direct third-party extension installation for normal users. A genuine one-click install requires publishing through the Chrome Web Store. Any random site claiming it can bypass that safely is misleading you.

Does “Likely AI” mean the video is definitely fake?

No. Sift reports an estimate based on available evidence. Platform labels can be wrong, disclosures can be missing, and community votes can disagree. Treat the score as a warning signal, not proof.

How do beta updates work?

Updates are manual for now. Download the newer ZIP, extract it over a stable folder, then click Reload for Sift on chrome://extensions. Chrome Web Store distribution will enable automatic updates later.

What information leaves my browser?

The hosted beta receives video identifiers, compact classification signals, and anonymous feedback. It does not receive full videos, audio, screenshots, or sampled video frames. See the beta privacy summary.
